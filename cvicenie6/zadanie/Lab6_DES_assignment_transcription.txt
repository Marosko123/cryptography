Exercise:

1) Write a program to implement a number of simplified DES operations. The information message is
encoded in accordance with the general scheme of the DES algorithm. The programs may be written in a
high-level language and implement DES operations in a simplified form, but they are simplified as follows:
 - the use of two rounds;
 - no modification of keys in each round;
 - the expansion operation is not converted.

Explanation (from the sheet):
The first most significant bit in the numbering of bits in Intel x86 – and we see it is the number 6 – goes to
the first place in the numbering of bits in the original DES – and this we see is the 58th place. That is, the bit
with index 6 maps to 58 in the DES IP table. In the final permutation table (IP^-1), the place 58 is in the second
row from the bottom and the seventh column. Therefore the bit “6” should be placed in this cell. It has to be in
the first one again so that we can decode the message correctly. Question on the sheet: where should our number 6 go?
We find the answer in the table of the final permutation: this place is 40 (since the number 40 itself is in that position).
